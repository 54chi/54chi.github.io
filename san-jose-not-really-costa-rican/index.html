San Jose, not really Costa Rican, but then again...

11 March 2016 on trips

First impression: it is very hot.
Second impression: it is very nice.
Third impression: they have Uber!!
Fourth impression: they have McDonald's too...and Starbucks, Subway, Applebee's, Carl's Jr, etc. etc. and Infosys and Indian restaurants...

So far, San Jose is like a warmer version of Miami, with less traffic, better/cheaper food (if you don't go to a chain) and much, much better coffee. On the minus side, alcohol is expensive (compared to USA), and their craft beers are not exactly very good (but they are better than the ones from Lima, IMHO)

What really excels here is the location to a bunch of nearby activities: The Caribbean and Pacific beaches, a bunch of volcanoes, waterfalls, white-water rafting and national reserves are all within a day trip.

E.g. Tomorrow I'm going to Poas, a waterfall and a coffee plantation tour. On Sunday, I am going to Tortuguero on the caribbean side, through the rainforest and banana plantations. On Monday, I want to either go to Manuel Antonio reserve on the Pacific ocean side, or the more famous Arenal volcano...still looking for a tour there.

I imagine people coming here to train for tomb raiding :)

And Zika? Haven't seen it yet.
